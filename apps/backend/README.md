#About project
RentalApp:
RentalApp is a web application designed to manage end-to-end rental property operations.
My role involved collaborating with clients in daily meetings to gather requirements and convert them into finalized wireframes aligned with business needs.

Initially, we built the system using a monolithic architecture and supported multiple user roles such as landlords, property managers, agents, vendors, and prospective tenants.
The workflow starts with property listing, where landlords can add property details like address, amenities, and tenancy information, and publish them publicly.

Landlords or managers can add properties with details like address, amenities, tenancy info, and descriptions.
They can also configure viewing slots using a calendar-based system with recurring options (daily, weekly, alternate days).
Each property is assigned a viewing agent responsible for coordinating with interested tenants.

We supported two types of viewings:
Open Home → unlimited attendees
Private Slots → restricted bookings with specific time slots

Prospective tenants can book, reschedule, or cancel their viewing slots.
Interested tenants submit a detailed renter profile including personal details, residential history, employment, income, references, and additional occupants.
They also complete documents like bond forms, tenancy agreements, and background/credit checks.
Based on the tenant profile, landlords or managers shortlist applicants and send payment links to finalize the process.
After payment, a move-in inspection is conducted to identify issues like plumbing or maintenance.
Tasks are created and assigned to service professionals, and can be broken into sub-tasks for better tracking.
Vendors provide quotations based on service categories and predefined price lists.
This helps standardize service costs and approvals.
A similar inspection process is followed during tenant move-out to assess damages and close the tenancy.

challenages:
One challenge we faced was managing complex workflows like scheduling and tenant screening, and we solved it by structuring modular flows within the monolithic system.
